Postdoctoral Researchers

Dr. Honghui Qi

Honghui Qi, a graduate with a Ph.D. degree from Tongji University in 2025. His doctoral research focused on the behavior of structures under fire conditions, including high-temperature material properties of concrete, fire resistance of concrete-filled steel tubular columns, structural collapse mechanisms of steel frames during fires, and intelligent early-warning systems. Currently, he joined UMD as a postdoctoral researcher, where his work fouces on wildfire-urban interface (WUI) fire risk assessment and fire safety in mass timber structures.

Dr. Ikwulono David Unobe

Postdoc Researcher

Dr. Ikwulono David Unobe obtained his masters degree in Civil Engineering from Idaho State University in 2014, and his PhD in Engineering and Applied Science, also from Idaho State University in 2018. He currently works as a post-doctoral research associate with the Civil and Environmental Engineering department at Utah State University. His research interests include structural health monitoring and non-destructive evaluation of tructural members, load-structure interaction under extreme and multi-hazard loading events and the development of data centric methods for evaluating damage to and residual capacities of in-service structural members. His work in this area has included the development of tools for remotely estimating the serviceability of traffic ancillary structures as well as the development of methodologies for estimating the residual capacity of structural members after extreme loading events. He also has extensive training in image and data analysis and has utilized this training to work on forensic problems of interest to the intelligence and criminal justice community including serial number restoration and the characterization of fire damage. His focus in this area has been on the development of data driven methodologies for the enhancement of forensic processes.
